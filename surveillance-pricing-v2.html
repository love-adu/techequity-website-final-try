The Problem

What is surveillance pricing?

AI that charges each customer a different price for the same product — based on your personal data.

Companies tag shoppers "impulsive buyer," "recently divorced," "first-time car buyer" and let algorithms set prices millions of times per day with no disclosure.

AB 2564 — what it does.

AB 2564 does not ban dynamic pricing or discounts. It bans the covert use of personal profiling data to extract maximum prices without consumers' knowledge.

Bans Willingness-to-Pay Pricing

Prohibits using personal behavioral and financial data to set individualized prices for identical goods.

Requires Disclosure

Companies must inform consumers when algorithmic pricing is in use and what categories of data drive price differences.

Ends Hidden Markup Schemes

Mandates a transparent base price. Discounts must be clearly disclosed and not secretly calibrated to each person's breaking point.

The Bottom Line California courts already confirmed pricing people based on personal vulnerability is illegal. AB 2564 makes that protection universal, proactive, and enforceable-- not just after the fact.

Industry Arguments (and the Answers)

"Dynamic pricing benefits consumers through discounts."
→
These programs inflate the base price first, then calibrate discounts to each shopper's breaking point.
"This is just supply and demand."
→
Demand-based pricing responds to markets. Surveillance pricing, on the other hand, responds to your personal data. Hiding discrimination behind algorithms is not a public good.
"Consumers can shop around."
→
Not when price changes are invisible. FTC found intermediaries use data "down to mouse movements." Amazon holds 40%+ of U.S. e-commerce.
"AB 2564 bans loyalty programs."
→
Wrong!! AB 2564 targets covert profiling-based pricing only. Senior and student discounts are disclosed and universal.

Why a one-pager works.

Legislators and their staff manage dozens of bills at once and are too busy to look at anything for more than a few minutes. A well-crafted one-pager is often the only document that gets read cover-to-cover. Ours is designed to be fully understood in under two minutes.

—Plain-language definition of surveillance pricing
—Key stats are ehanhced in size and bolded
—Three real court cases with dollar amounts
—What AB 2564 does and what it does NOT ban
